Getting in Touch

Evince hackers use the mailing list and irc channel #evince on the server irc.gnome.org for keeping in touch and discussing issues regarding the project.

The TODO List

Evince has a TODO list that should be updated frequently describing the work that the developers would like to undertake and will gladly accept patches for.

Debugging and Profiling

Evince has awesome debugging and profiling support built into the code. Refer to the debugging.txt document in SVN under doc/debugging.txt.

The Bug Lists

Look here for the correct bugzilla queries to find Evince bugs, each list also describes it's query attributes. So if you're expecting a bug to be a release blocker, make sure it has the proper attributes to appear on the release blocker list.

  • Release Blocker
    Status: NEW or ASSIGNED or REOPENED or NEEDINFO or VERIFIED
    Severity: blocker
    Priority: Immediate
  • New Crashers
    Status: UNCONFIRMED
    Severity: critical or major
    Priority: Urgent or High
  • Known Crashers
    Status: NEW or ASSIGNED or REOPENED or NEEDINFO or VERIFIED
    Severity: critical or major
    Priority: Urgent or High
  • Un-reviewed Patches
    Status: _any_
    Severity: _any_
    Priority: _any_
    Patch Status: None
  • Easy Fixes
    Status: UNCONFIRMED or NEW or ASSIGNED or REOPENED or NEEDINFO or VERIFIED
    Severity: trivial
    Priority: _any_
  • Features Planned for Current or Next Release
    Status: UNCONFIRMED or NEW or ASSIGNED or REOPENED or NEEDINFO or VERIFIED
    Severity: enhancement
    Priority: Normal
  • Long Term Future Enhancements
    Status: UNCONFIRMED or NEW or ASSIGNED or REOPENED or NEEDINFO or VERIFIED
    Severity: enhancement
    Priority: Low
