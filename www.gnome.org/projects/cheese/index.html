What is Cheese?

Cheese is a GNOME application designed to take photos and videos of you and your friends with your webcam, add special effects to them and share them with your friends and family. It was written as part of Google's 2007 Summer of Code lead by daniel g. siegel and mentored by Raphaël Slinckx, and has most of the classical photo booth features after a bare couple of months of development. Under the hood, Cheese uses GStreamer to apply fancy effects to photos and videos. After a success of the Summer of Code, the development continued and we still are looking for people with nice ideas and patches ;)

Effects

Just bought a webcam? That's boring, it's much more fun morphing yourself into Hulk or a nice pink alien, or even pile up effects in order to obtain the most amazing results! Right now, you can for instance apply the following effects to photos and videos:

  • Mauve
  • Noir/Blanc
  • Saturation
  • Hulk
  • Shagadelic
  • Vertical Flip
  • Horizontal Flip
  • Vertigo
  • Edge
  • Dice
  • Warp

Scene 42, action!

In addition to photos, Cheese allows you to record videos. Even there you can record them with effects. So Mr. Kubrick, go ahead and present us your new Hollywood-movie!

Share

Finished taking pictures and movies? Or doesn't your makeup look good? Then go ahead and share your files to your friends and family! It's already possible to export photos to F-Spot and upload them to Flickr, mail them or use them as your account photo in GNOME. Of course you can save it anywhere on your disk. Future releases will provide bindings to Conduit, which will allow you to share your photos and videos to many more places, e.g. setting the Facebook account photo or putting your Hulk-like photo on your iPod!

Future

The project isn't stopping here. We are already looking forward, and an integration with Conduit already in the works will permit exchanging online photos and videos as well as deep integration with the desktop. The software will also use OpenGL to speed-up image and video processing, enabling us to add real-time effects as well as eye-candy. We are already considering features such as live incrustation of props and backgrounds, motion tracking, automatic hackergotchi creation, raffle mode and much more.. If you want to know more, have a look at our crazy ideas and a very ambitious roadmap.

What are the Requirements?

  • GNOME 2.20
  • GStreamer 0.10
  • postr for Flickr export (optional)
  • f-spot for F-Spot export (optional)
  • a webcam
  • a brain

For a more detailed list of requirements, have a look at the FAQ
