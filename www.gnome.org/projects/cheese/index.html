What is Cheese?

Cheese is a GNOME application designed taking photos and videos of you and your friends with your webcam, add special effects to them and share them with your friends and family. It was written as part of Google's 2007 Summer of Code, and has most of the classical photo booth features after a bare couple of months of development. Under the hood, Cheese uses GStreamer to apply fancy effects to photos and videos.

What are the Requirements?

  • glib-2.0 >= 2.12.0
  • gobject-2.0
  • gtk+-2.0 >= 2.10.0
  • libglade-2.0 >= 2.0.0
  • gdk-2.0
  • dbus-1
  • gstreamer-0.10 >= 0.10.12
  • gstreamer-plugins-base-0.10 >= 0.10.12
  • gstreamer-plugins-good-0.10 >= 0.10.12
  • gstreamer-pango-0.10 >= 0.10.12
  • gnome-vfs-2.0
  • cairo
  • libgnomeui-2.0
  • xf86vidmodeproto
  • libebook-1.2 (evolution-data-server)
  • postr for Flickr export (optional)
  • f-spot for F-Spot export (optional)
  • a webcam (a v4l2-webcam is highly recommended)
  • a brain
