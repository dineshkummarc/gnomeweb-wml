What are the conduits

A conduit is a plugin for the Gnome-Pilot daemon, which handles synchronization of a database on the pilot (eg. DateDB, the calendardatabase) with a local program/database (eg. GnomeCal, the Gnome calendar program).

Conduits come in various flavors, two systems conduits for backing up data and installing files, custom conduits and conduits of the SyncAbs type.

En example is the GnomeCal conduit, a SyncAbs conduit. It is responsible for matching pilot records against local entries, comparing them for differences, converting to/from pilot record format.

The Email conduit is a custom conduit. It is not concerned with record states, its only concern is copying data to/from a database.

If you're planning to write a conduit to synchronize your favorite application with your pilot device, you'll have to decide on the type of conduit, normally the choice will be limited to a custom conduit or a SyncAbs conduit.
